Real-Time Speech Enhancement on Edge Devices Guided by Harmonic and Voice-Activity Cues Utilizing Skin-Attachable Accelerometer

Yonghun Song, Yeongmin Kim, Yunsik Kim, Yeeun Kim, and Yoonyoung Chung*.

Paper Code (GitHub) Dataset (TAPS)

Abstract

Skin-attachable accelerometers (ACCs) measure noise-robust speech vibrations, complementing acoustic microphones (AMs). However, multimodal models fuse both signals with parallel encoders or attention blocks, which hinders edge deployment. We present a lightweight ACC-assisted U-Net v2 (LAU-NetV2) for speech enhancement based on efficient cue-guided feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM). From ACC, we derive speech-informative cues: temporal voice activity to suppress noise and spectral harmonics to emphasize voiced structures. These cues condition intermediate U-Net feature maps via FiLM to selectively control time-frequency representations. The FiLM module adds only 30k parameters, yielding a total of 46k—68× fewer than heavy multimodal models. Integrating ACC cues improves PESQ from 1.78 to 2.78 on the TAPS+DNS dataset and outperforms lightweight baselines. Finally, our wearable prototype with the LAU-NetV2 runs in 48.66 ms on a microcontroller, enabling real-time voice interfaces.
